# BuildLocal.agency Content Strategy
## Sprint 1 Deliverable — March 2026

**Goal:** 300+ indexed pages targeting long-tail, high-intent keywords to generate 10 qualified inbound leads per day through organic search.

**Current state:** 15 pages live. **Target state:** 350+ pages across industry hubs, city hubs, industry+city landing pages, blog posts, comparison pages, resource pages, and case studies.

## 1. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Trade-Specific Website Solutions
**Rationale:** The core commercial pillar. Every trade has different pain points, customer expectations, and seasonal patterns. A roofing company's website needs are fundamentally different from a plumber's. This pillar positions BuildLocal as the expert who understands each trade, not a generic web agency.
**Connection to services:** Directly feeds Website Design, Website Management, and SEO/Local Search service pages. Each industry hub links to the relevant service offering.

### Pillar 2: Local Market Dominance
**Rationale:** Trades businesses operate in specific geographies. This pillar targets business owners searching for web design help in their city and educates them on what it takes to rank locally. It powers the city hub and industry+city pages.
**Connection to services:** Drives SEO & Local Search, Google Business Profile services. Each city page references local competition data and market-specific insights.

### Pillar 3: Website ROI & Business Growth for Trades
**Rationale:** The awareness and consideration pillar. Many trades business owners are skeptical about websites or have been burned before. This pillar educates on the financial case: cost per lead, customer lifetime value, website vs. lead gen platforms, and measurable results.
**Connection to services:** Supports Pricing page, SEO ROI Calculator, and case studies. Builds trust by showing real numbers.

### Pillar 4: Digital Marketing Without the BS
**Rationale:** Trades owners are busy, practical people who hate jargon and empty promises. This pillar covers comparison content (BuildLocal vs. competitors, DIY vs. professional), honest assessments of what works and what doesn't, and practical guides. It differentiates BuildLocal's voice from corporate agency speak.
**Connection to services:** Powers comparison pages, feeds FAQ content, supports the "no contracts, flat pricing" differentiator.

## 2. Keyword Pattern Decision

### Recommended Primary Pattern: `[trade] website design [city]`

**Analysis of options:**

| Pattern | Pros | Cons | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| `websites for [trade] in [city]` | Natural language, conversational | Lower commercial intent, informational framing | Secondary use in body copy |
| `[trade] web design [city]` | Short, punchy | "Web design" is slightly dated, less specific | Acceptable alternate |
| `[trade] website design [city]` | High commercial intent, matches buyer language, aligns with existing service page ("website-design") | Slightly longer | **Primary pattern** |
| `[trade] website builder [city]` | — | Implies DIY tools, wrong intent | Reject |

**Rationale:**

1. **Search intent alignment.** A business owner searching "[trade] website design [city]" has commercial intent — they want to hire someone. This matches BuildLocal's conversion goal.
2. **Consistency with existing site language.** The current service page is `/services/website-design`. The existing meta titles use "Web Design" phrasing. `Website design` maintains consistency while being more specific than `web design`.
3. **Competition gap.** Most competitors target generic `web design [city]` or `small business website [city]`. The trade-specific prefix (`roofing`, `plumbing`, etc.) targets a long-tail niche with significantly lower competition and higher intent.
4. **Title tag format:** `[Trade] Website Design [City] | Websites for [Trade Plural] — BuildLocal`
   - Example: `Roofing Website Design Phoenix | Websites for Roofers — BuildLocal`

**Secondary keyword patterns to include in content:**
- `[trade] web design [city]` (close variant)
- `website for [trade plural] in [city]` (natural language variant for body copy)
- `[trade] website cost` (informational, for FAQ sections)
- `best website for [trade] business` (comparison/consideration)

## Appendix C: Unique Local Context Framework for Industry+City Pages

To avoid thin content and template-swapping, every industry+city page must include at least 3 of the following unique local elements:

1. **Climate/weather demand driver** — e.g., Phoenix monsoon season drives roofing demand; extreme heat drives HVAC demand year-round
2. **Population/growth data** — e.g., Buckeye is the #1 fastest-growing city in the US; Gilbert grew 13% in 5 years
3. **Housing stock characteristics** — e.g., Scottsdale has 40% custom homes; Mesa has older neighborhoods with aging pipes
4. **Local competitor count** — e.g., "There are 340 roofing companies in Phoenix. Only 45% have a website."
5. **Median home value** — correlates to job ticket size; Scottsdale ($650K) vs. Buckeye ($380K) changes the framing
6. **Local regulations or licensing** — e.g., Arizona ROC (Registrar of Contractors) licensing requirements
7. **Seasonal patterns** — e.g., HVAC demand peaks May-September in Phoenix; landscaping peaks in spring
8. **Community characteristics** — e.g., Fountain Hills is a retirement-heavy community; Tempe is college-adjacent

This framework ensures that `/industries/roofing/phoenix` reads substantively different from `/industries/roofing/tucson` — not just city-name swaps.
